FAQ · Frequently Asked Questions

The questions you'd feel awkward asking.

Ten common things new joiners want to know but rarely raise out loud — dress codes, late arrivals, lunch reimbursements, lost cards, cross-office visits. Answers below; if anything's unclear, your six contacts in Section 05 of the main portal are the human source of truth.

Questions 10 · Read time ~6 min · Last updated May 2026
01 What should I wear on Day 1?

Both Indicium AI is smart casual as the default. Jeans + a clean top + comfortable shoes work for almost any day in the office. No formal dress code, no suit, no tie required.

The exception is client-facing days — see Q9 below for that. On your very first day, you'll spend most of your time inside our office with internal colleagues, so erring on the casual end is perfectly fine. If you feel awkward, leaning slightly smart on Day 1 never hurts — you'll quickly calibrate by what your team wears.

02 What if I'm late on Day 1?

Don't panic — public transport delays happen. Send a quick Slack or email to your Office Manager as soon as you know:

London ping Lou on Slack.
Lisbon ping Carla on Slack.

If you don't have Slack set up yet (which is likely on Day 1), email works. We'll reschedule your tour and onboarding sequence — nothing's lost. A late start by 15–30 minutes is genuinely no big deal here. Just communicate.

03 Can I park nearby?

London Driving is strongly discouraged. The office sits inside the Congestion Charge zone and the Ultra Low Emission Zone (ULEZ), so daily driving adds up fast. Parking around Liverpool Street is scarce and expensive (£40+ per day in nearby NCP car parks). Take the Tube, Overground, Elizabeth Line, or National Rail — Liverpool Street station is literally a 30-second walk from the office.

Lisbon Limited and paid. Street parking around Marquês de Pombal is paid (EMEL meters) and tends to fill up quickly. There are several paid garages in the area (around €15–25/day). The Metro at Marquês de Pombal is the fastest route — one minute walk and connects two lines (Blue + Yellow). Most colleagues use Metro, GIRA bikes, or scooters.

04 How do I expense lunch with a client?

Both Client-facing lunches are typically expensable — keep the receipt and submit it through the standard expense process. The exact tool and limit depend on your engagement; Tom (Operations) is the right person to confirm both.

Solo / internal-only lunches are normally not reimbursed. The grey area is team lunches with internal colleagues — for those, check with your Engagement Lead or Tom before assuming. Better to ask once than to submit a receipt that gets rejected.

05 Can I visit the other office?

Both Yes — cross-office visits are encouraged. London ↔ Lisbon swaps happen regularly, and it's a great way to meet people from the other half of Europe.

How to set it up:

1. Check with Tom on project coverage and any expense policy for the trip.
2. Message the destination Office Manager (Lou for London, Carla for Lisbon) at least a few days ahead so they can set up a hot desk and access for you.
3. Book your own travel through the usual expense process — flights, train, accommodation. Confirm what's covered with Tom first.

Visits typically run 2–5 working days. Longer stays are possible for specific projects.

06 Are there vegetarian, halal, or gluten-free options nearby?

London Excellent variety. Spitalfields Market (5-min walk) has dedicated vegan stalls, halal kebabs, gluten-free wraps. Broadgate Circle has Beany Green (lots of vegetarian options), Franco Manca (gluten-free pizza on request), and Crussh (juices, salads, GF wraps). Most chains around Liverpool Street label allergens clearly.

Lisbon Solid options. Cotidiano (5-min) has vegetarian and gluten-free menus marked. A Padaria Portuguesa on the square has clearly-labelled allergens and several vegetarian sandwiches. For Asian / Vietnamese vegetarian, Tre Bambu has plenty of options. Halal is rarer in the immediate area — central Lisbon has more options if you walk slightly further.

If you have a strict dietary requirement (severe allergy, kosher, etc.), let Lou/Carla know — they can help plan office events around it.

07 What if I lose my access card?

Both Don't panic. Ping your Office Manager on Slack as soon as you realise:

London Lou arranges a replacement and a temporary day-pass while you wait. The building reception can also issue a same-day visitor pass if she's not around.

Lisbon Carla handles the replacement. The Monday building's front desk can also issue a temporary card while she sorts the new one.

Replacements typically take 1–2 working days. There's no charge for the first replacement; multiple losses in a short period may have a small fee depending on the building's policy.

08 WiFi isn't working — who do I call?

Both Declan is your first call — he's IT support and handles network, hardware, and access issues. Ping him on Slack (see Section 05 of the main portal for his Slack link).

While you wait, two quick self-checks that often solve it:

1. Forget the network and rejoin using the password you got from Lou/Carla on Day 1. If you don't remember it, ask them to share it again.
2. Try the guest network for a short-term fix — it's slower but unblocks you for emails and Slack. Lou/Carla can share the guest credentials.

If the WiFi is down for the whole office (not just your laptop), Lou/Carla will already know and will be coordinating with the building. No need to also ping them.

09 What's the dress code for client meetings?

Both Smart casual to business casual, calibrated to the client. Default: smart trousers/dark jeans, a clean shirt or polo, closed shoes. No tie, no suit needed for most engagements.

The exception is banking, insurance, and energy clients — they tend to dress more formally. For those, add a blazer or jacket and skip the jeans. Your Engagement Lead will flag if the client expects something more formal; if you're not sure, ask them before the meeting.

Rule of thumb: match the client one notch up. If they wear business casual, you wear business casual. Avoid hoodies, branded t-shirts, and trainers for client-facing days. When in doubt, dress slightly smarter than your average office day.

10 Is there a shower at the office?

Lisbon Yes. The Monday building has a 24h gym with showers, accessible with your office card. Bring your own towel. This is also great for cycling commuters or post-lunch runs in Eduardo VII park.

London Yes — the building has showers and lockers as part of its end-of-trip facilities, designed for cycling and running commuters. Bike storage is on-site, and fresh towels are usually provided (check with Lou for towel availability that day).

Both offices genuinely support active commuting and lunch-break workouts. Use it.
